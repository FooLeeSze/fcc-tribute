Intersting facts about cats

  • Cats have excellent night vision and can see at only one-sixth the light level required for human vision.
  • A raised tail indicates a friendly greeting, and flattened ears indicates hostility.
  • Post-nursing cats often purr as a sign of contentment: when being petted, becoming relaxed, or eating.
  • Cats have relatively few taste buds compared to humans (470 or so versus more than 9,000 on the human tongue).
  • Playing mimics hunting and is important in helping kittens learn to stalk, capture, and kill prey.
  • A cat falling from heights of up to 3 meters (9.8 ft) can right itself and land on its paws.
  • Cats can detect ultrasound, which enables it to detect ultrasonic calls made by rodent prey.
  • To aid with navigation and sensation, cats have dozens of movable whiskers (vibrissae) over their body, especially their faces.
  • Domestic cats' scent rubbing behavior toward humans or other cats is thought to be a feline means for social bonding.
  • The claws on a cat's fore feet are typically sharper than those on the hind feet.
